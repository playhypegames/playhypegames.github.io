Frequently asked questions

When is Drift Loop coming out?

Soon! Drift Loop is on its way to the App Store. The fastest way to hear about it is to add your email above — we'll message you the moment it goes live.

How will in-app purchases work?

Drift Loop will be free to play. There are optional in-app purchases — coins and a one-time Remove Ads — all processed securely by Apple through the App Store using your existing Apple Account. We never see or store your card details.

How do I restore my purchases?

If you reinstall the game or switch devices and don't see something you bought, open Settings inside Drift Loop and tap Restore Purchases. As long as you're signed in with the same Apple Account you purchased with, your Remove Ads and coin packs will come right back — at no additional charge.

Can I get a refund?

Refunds for App Store purchases are handled by Apple, not by us. Go to reportaproblem.apple.com, sign in with your Apple Account, find your Drift Loop purchase, and choose Request a refund. If you have any trouble, email us and we'll help.

What kind of ads will Drift Loop show?

Drift Loop has zero banner ads and zero interstitial ads. The only ads are optional rewarded videos that you choose to watch in exchange for a bonus (like extra coins or a continue), served through Google AdMob. Prefer none at all? The one-time Remove Ads purchase turns them off completely.

Will my progress and unlocks be saved?

Yes. Your progress, coins, and unlocked cars, drift smoke, and sky palettes are saved on your device and — if you have iCloud enabled for Drift Loop — synced to your Apple Account so they can follow you between devices. If you play with Game Center, your best scores are tied to your Game Center profile. If something looks off, email us with your device model and iOS version.
